About Me

I'm Emily O'Grady, a marine data scientist and ocean researcher passionate about bridging the gap between cutting-edge technology and ocean science. Currently pursuing my Masters of Digital Innovation at Dalhousie University, I combine my deep expertise in oceanographic data management with emerging technologies like AI and machine learning to advance our understanding of marine ecosystems.

At the Bedford Institute of Oceanography, I lead initiatives in data quality control, database management, and open science practices. I've authored R packages used nationally for oceanographic data processing, chair the R & Git Support Group, and have contributed to numerous scientific publications. My work spans from developing AI solutions for ocean science to managing long-term monitoring program data and ensuring data accessibility through international platforms.

I'm particularly interested in the intersection of responsible AI, science communication, and collaborative research. Whether I'm presenting at conferences, mentoring colleagues in R and Git workflows, or developing innovative data visualization tools, I'm driven by a commitment to making ocean science more accessible, reproducible, and impactful.

When I'm not analyzing oceanographic data or exploring new digital tools, I enjoy connecting with the broader scientific community through workshops, seminars, and professional networks. I believe in the power of collaboration and knowledge sharing to drive scientific innovation forward.
